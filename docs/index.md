# Welcome to SSGS Wiki!

## :material-information: General Notes

!!! info "About This Project"

    Sacred Gold and Storm Silver are the fourth set of ROM hacks by Drayano, following Fire Red Omega, Spirit Gold, and Blaze Black/Volt White.

    Unlike the previous Spirit Gold hack, Sacred Gold and Storm Silver take full advantage of DS scripting tools, featuring extensive new scripted events and aesthetic changes to deliver a content-rich and visually enhanced experience.

Building on the foundation of HeartGold and SoulSilver, these hacks maintain the original maps and storyline while introducing significant gameplay enhancements:

- All 493 Generation IV Pokémon are obtainable within the game
- Increased difficulty with revamped trainer rosters
- Pokémon stat adjustments, new abilities, and expanded movesets
- Self-contained evolution system (no trading required)
- New scripted events and legendary encounters

!!! warning "Version Notes"

    Sacred Gold and Storm Silver come in two versions: **Complete** (with Pokémon buffs and changes) and **Classic** (vanilla Pokémon data). Choose the version that fits your preferred gameplay style.

## :material-download: Patching Info

!!! success "Quick Start"

    Patch a **clean Pokémon HeartGold or SoulSilver ROM** that you've legally obtained.

!!! danger "Important Warnings"

    - Save frequently, especially around the Safari Zone area
    - Some events may not work properly on certain flash cards (R4, etc.)
    - The Celebi event at Tohjo Falls is known to freeze in some versions

For detailed patching instructions, see the [Patching Guide](getting_started/patching_guide.md).

## :material-star-four-points: Feature List

=== "Pokémon & Encounters"

    **Complete Pokédex**

    - All **493 Pokémon** from Generation IV available
    - National Dex received immediately upon getting the Pokédex
    - Almost all Pokémon available before the first Elite Four challenge
    - Minor encounter differences between Sacred Gold and Storm Silver

    **Wild Encounters**

    - Revamped wild Pokémon distribution
    - Explained in-game by a "migration wave" to the Johto and Kanto regions
    - Strategic placement of rare Pokémon throughout the game

=== "Battles & Difficulty"

    **Trainer Battles**

    - Completely revamped trainer rosters with greater variety
    - Gym Leaders and Elite Four use full teams of **six Pokémon**
    - Significantly increased difficulty curve
    - New optional trainer battles added:
        - Battle Ethan/Lyra
        - Challenge Cynthia
        - Face off against Steven

    **Boss Battles**

    - Gym Leaders have heavily boosted teams
    - Elite Four members at championship-level difficulty
    - Important trainers use competitive strategies

=== "Pokémon Changes"

    **Complete Version Edits**

    - Modified stats, abilities, and types for many Pokémon
    - Expanded TM compatibility
    - Adjusted experience rates for easier training
    - Additional level-up moves for better movepool coverage

    **Classic Version**

    - Pokémon retain their original data
    - Perfect for players who prefer the vanilla experience
    - All other game features remain intact

=== "Moves & Evolution"

    **Move Updates**

    - All Black & White move changes applied where possible
    - HM moves (Cut, Strength, Rock Smash) buffed for usefulness
    - Extensive level-up movepool additions

    **Evolution System**

    - **No trading required** - completely self-contained
    - Trade evolution items work like Evolution Stones
    - New items added for location-based evolutions:
        - Leafeon and Glaceon
        - Magneton → Magnezone
        - Nosepass → Probopass

=== "Items & Convenience"

    **Item Availability**

    - All evolution items obtainable before first Elite Four round
    - All TMs available before first Elite Four challenge
    - **EXP Share** available in Violet City (early game!)
    - Evolution stones purchasable in shops
    - Coins can be purchased at Goldenrod Game Corner

    **Quality of Life**

    - Most unnecessary Cut trees removed
    - Reduced annoying HM requirements
    - Gym Leader phone numbers given directly after battles
    - Buyable Game Corner coins
    - Optimized game flow

=== "New Events & Scripts"

    **Scripted Events**

    - Early **Eevee** gift from Cynthia
    - Elite Four teleport system (challenge in any order, B/W style)
    - Team Rocket takeover of Route 47 and Safari Zone
    - **Shellder and Cloyster siege** in Fuchsia City
    - Custom legendary encounters (not random wild battles)
    - Revamped Game Corner prizes (Porygon in Goldenrod!)

    **In-Game Trades**

    - New and edited trades throughout the game
    - Obtain hard-to-find Pokémon: Gligar, Skarmory, Beldum, Smoochum
    - Traded Pokémon come with special TM moves

=== "Aesthetic Changes"

    **Visual Enhancements**

    - Karen's new battle background
    - Lance uses Cynthia's DPPt background
    - Hoenn and Sinnoh overworld sprites added
    - DP trainers make appearances
    - Black & White sprites integrated (especially Hoenn Pokémon)
    - Main Pokémon have custom seals for personalization
    - Decapitalized Pokémon names (Pidgeotto vs PIDGEOTTO)
    - Some dialogue restored to GSC versions

## :material-map-marker-path: Story & Progression

**Enhanced Level Curve**

Sacred Gold and Storm Silver address the original games' poor level curve through strategic blockades that increase linearity:

- Forced to visit Cianwood before Mahogany Town
- Kanto region follows a specific route for clear objectives
- Results in more balanced level progression
- Better pacing throughout the adventure

**Additional Story Content**

!!! note "Legendary Trainer Quest"

    A new side story focuses on the concept of a "Legendary Trainer" who can meet the "creator," involving the hunt for all Plate items. This feature is still being developed and will be expanded in future releases.

## :material-file-document-multiple: Complete vs Classic

Not everyone wants buffed Pokémon or type changes. That's why two versions exist!

=== "Complete"

    The default version with **custom buffs and changes** to Pokémon:

    - Modified abilities and stats
    - Type combination changes
    - Enhanced movesets
    - Rebalanced BST distributions

    **Recommended for:** Players who want the full enhanced experience

=== "Classic"

    For purists who prefer the original Pokémon data:

    - Pokémon use their **original Gen IV stats and types**
    - No custom ability changes
    - Standard movesets preserved
    - All other game features remain active

    **Recommended for:** Players who prefer vanilla Pokémon mechanics
